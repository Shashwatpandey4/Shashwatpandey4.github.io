I’ve been trying to write a blog for years but never really managed to organize my thoughts effectively. When I read other people's blogs, some appear straightforward, like they’re simply having a casual conversation about an experience or explaining how to do something.

Then, I came across blogs like those of Chris Olah. The way he explained the concept of convolutions using the analogy of a dropped ball left a profound impression on me. It made me realize that this was how I used to approach understanding concepts, not anywhere near Chris's level, of course, but striving to grasp things in a simple, intuitive way.

In general, blogs about convolutions often echo one another or follow the content of Andrew Ng's Coursera course. While there's nothing inherently wrong with that, the point is that those writers often reiterate perspectives already expressed. If I’m going to write,  I need to articulate my own thoughts and present a fresh perspective, rather than simply echoing what’s already been said.

With this realization, I’ve decided to make this reflection the subject of my first blog. I’ll try to follow my intuition and write authentically, by sharing my personal experiences, insights, and the unique ways I interpret concepts, without overly conforming to existing narratives. This won’t be intended for an audience; it will simply be a blog, a personal expression of my thoughts.
